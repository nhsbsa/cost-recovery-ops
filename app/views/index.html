Version 41 / S1/S072 Average cost UK Claims, Contestations and Resubmissions, and Update person and entitlement details (August 2025 - Present)

S1/S072 Average cost claims (MVP Version 1)
- Interaction Designer: Megan Bell In this version you will find an improved version of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions, specifically the Partial maintain and withdraw user journey.

For site flows, wireframes, and general design thinking for S1 Claims (MVP Version 1) visit the Content and Design Miro board (Password 'NHSBSA2025').

What we created:

  • Various content improvements throughout the resubmissions process.
  • Streamlined and improved the Partially maintain and withdraw journey by removing the Enter the number of months partially maintained and withdrawn screen (opens a previous prototype iteration) by instead asking users to enter the revised accounting period (which is the period of months they wish to maintain) and enter the total number of months all on one screen. We anticipate this will remove complexity involved in entering the revised accounting period, and ensuring the total number of maintained and withdrawn months calculates correctly.
  • Added linked claims and resubmissions table to the individual S1 entitlement on a person's record in the Entitlements and treatments section.
  • Added inset text to the confirm invoices you want to add to the resubmission screen guiding users to add invoices if the scenario where they have removed all previously selected/added invoices from the resubmission shell
  • Improved update person details journeys including First and last names, Current address, Date of birth and additional details including Sex, Nationality, UK National Insurance Number (NINO) and Contact number.
  • Improved update entitlement details journeys including First and last names, Date of birth, Personal identification number (PIN), and Date of residency in UK.

What we plan to test:

  • The improved resubmission journey with key focus on content tweaks and the streamlined Partially maintain and withdraw journey.
  • The Entitlements and treatments screen where we display all claims and resubmissions an individual S1 is linked to.
  • The interface displaying the inset text and 'Add invoices' button in the scenario where the user has removed all previously selected/added invoices from the resubmission shell.
  • All improved update person details journeys.
  • All improved update entitlement details journeys.

Tasks include:

  1. Firstly, search for the claim by ID (100005)
  2. View details of the first resubmission (optional)
  3. Create a new resubmission shell for claim 100005, and then add invoices to the resubmission
  4. Review the invoices within the resubmission including adding the reason for contestation, uploading any evidence, and investigating the invoices and entitlement details to make a decision to either Maintain, Withdraw or Partially maintain and withdraw.
  5. From an individual invoice and their details, navigate to their entitlements and treatments on their person record. Open up the S1 entitlement and review the linked claims and resubmissions associated with this entitlement.

What we found:

  • Including the total number of contested months on the Claim summary is technically complex because we don't record the number of contested months per individual invoice, therefore, this data has been removed.
  • All users could correctly explain the difference between 'Claimed' and 'Remaining' values on the Claim invoices screen - including when an invoice is only showing 'Claimed' values meaning that they've yet to be in a completed resubmission. They found this useful, particularly when completing Decision S11 work, or responding to DHSC queries and needing a quick 'at-a-glance' view of an individual invoice's value within the overall claim. Users could also understand the design rationale, given the limitations we had in designing the screen effectively.
  • As part of this prototype iteration, a decision was made to add the Acknowledgement letter back onto the Confirmation resubmission created screen, however, in UR, users pointed out that the 'Date the acknowledgement letter was sent to the member state' (required input on the Create a new resubmission screen) may not be accurate, particularly when dealing with postal resubmissions because they are asked to enter this date before actually being able to download and send the Acknowledgement letter which appears on the confirmation screen. For RINA resubmissions, there is no risk because the acknowledgement is sent immediately after the Claims Officer begins working the resubmission - it does not require them to find and download the letter, meaning that, users should have sent the acknowledgement prior to creating a new resubmission shell on the MOET system. Stakeholders are, however, aware of the risk involed with postal resubs and agree that the process will be improved post-MVP.
  • Users expected that the 'Months' and 'Accounting period' shown on the Invoices within resubmission screen would, at first, only show the latest/current value that each individual invoice is sat at i.e., if it's the first resub, it would be the original, claimed value, or if it was the fourth resub, it would be the remaining value after completing the third resubmission. Once users 'worked' each invoice, they would then see the 'Previous' and 'Remaining' values, allowing them to clearly see what decision had been made, and the amount of months maintained/withdrawn. This would also enable users to see which invoices are still to be worked, as not only would they only show a single value, but their status would still be sat as 'Contested'.
  • Users do not need to see the 'original' claimed value on the Invoices within resubmission screen, they instead, would expect to find this information within the individual Invoice details or on the overall Claim invoices screen.
  • Users really liked the improved display of previous claim and resubmission values within the individual Maintain/Withdraw/Partial journeys. Users said that they like that it's tucked away within a details component it avoids initially cluttering screens when they first land on them, but if they feel they need access to this information during these journeys, they know they can reveal this information when they need it.
  • Once Cost Recovery have cancelled an S1 entitlement, Claims Officers will make the decision against the invoice. This step is particularly important during the Partial journey as we display the entitlement start and end date on the first screen in that journey to help users correctly enter the revised accounting period. Users understood this additional information and confirmed it would help them enter the correct dates, albeit they did question why they needed to enter the revised accounting period if it's 9/10 times going to match the entitlement start and end date.
  • Most users correctly input the revised accounting period within the Partial journey. Some users were still a bit unsure on the exact dates they should be inputting as they know in their minds that we can only claim in whole months which meant that where we'd expect them to input the accounting period end date as the day after a person died, for example, they're actually inputting the last day of the month. However, users expressed that with training and time, they would become familiar with the correct process and system functionality.
  • At the time of testing the Partial journey, on the CYA screen, we were displaying the Revised accounting period (Period of maintained months), the withdrawn months, and the reason, withdrawal source, and optional additional comments - users said that they're happy to see that the total number of withdrawn months was automatically calculated by the system, based on the revised accounting period they'd input. If this total number of withdrawn months didn't look correct, they'd know they'd need to change the revised accounting period. Additionally, they also said they'd only need to be shown either the total number of maintained or total number of withdrawn months because both would be overkill, and they would be able to do some simple math to work out one or the other. However, after testing, the development team realised it would be significant effort to build the system so that it automatically calculates the total number of maintained and withdrawn months based on whatever revised accounting period the user enters. Instead, users will have to enter the total number of months they want to maintain alongside the revised accounting period, therefore, the UCD team agreed to remove all withdrawn-related info from this journey so that users only focus on what is being maintained to align with RINA. We also understand that users can very simply calculate what has been withdrawn using previous values and maintained values to find out what was withdrawn per resubmission.
  • Once a decision has been made against an individual invoice and users go back into the invoice details screen, users largely understand the information on display i.e., Original, previously maintained, and remaining values. However, some users did say that it could be made clearer that the 'previously maintained' values come directly from the last resubmission, rather than the entire claim. The UCD team, however, agreed that adding additional words such as 'Previously maintained months from latest resubmission' just adds additional words to an already busy screen. Instead, we hope that with training and use of the system, users will quickly understand what 'Previously maintained' is referring to.
  • Users like that we now display all claims and resubmissions linked to S1 entitlements as it will help them complete Decision S11 work, as well as correctly process Credit Notes by being able to clearly see all claims and resubs that they need to credit back to the Member State. This said, we initially designed the interface so that both the Claim ID and Invoice ID were links. The Claim ID redirects users to the individual claim, landing on the Claim summary screen whereas the Invoice ID could only redirect users to the Claim invoices screen because we show individual invoices per resubmission so it would be impossible to link to an individual invoice per person which covers all resubmissions. As a result of users testing these links, we found that users would prefer if instead of linking to the Claim invoices screen, if instead we could link to the individual resubmissions linked to the entitlement. This would speed up various processes for users as it reduces the need for them to search for the individual resubmissions.
  • Overall, changes made to content and design from the previous prototype iteration were very well received, enabling users to access the information they need throughout key areas in the resubmissions process, allowing them to complete tasks more efficiently. Users acknowledge that with time, and training, they will become familiar with new processes and system functionality, and can see the benefits these will have in their day-to-day work.

  • Users could not think of a reason why they would remove every invoice from the Confirm you want to add these invoices to the resubmission screen. They would likely only ever remove a select few invoices from that screen only if/when they realise they might have made a mistake e.g., selecting the wrong invoice in the first instance.
  • Users would not click the 'Continue' button on the Confirm you want to add these invoices to the resubmission screen if they had removed all invoices from the resubmission shell because it does not make sense to them to continue if there are no invoices in the resubmission shell.
  • If users wanted to return back to the empty resubmission shell, they would use the breadcrumbs to do this.
  • In the edge case scenario where an invoice has been previously fully withdrawn, users would prefer if the 'Partial' button was disabled rather than completely removed due to the fact that they used to the current button layout/user interface. They said removing the 'Partial' button might instead cause confusion or they might think something was wrong with the system so keeping the button but preventing users from being able to click/go down the 'Partial' route prevents users from making a mistake.

  • Users are not used to identifying entitlements by ID so would like the entitlement start and end dates within each heading on the Case history - Entitlements screen to help them correctly locate the correct entitlement when viewing updates.
  • Users sometimes receive contact numbers containing the country/area code. Rather than making this mandatory to enter, or not asking users to enter it at all on the Add or Change additional personal details screens, instead we should make the hint text clearer so that users should enter the country/area code only if they know it.

What we found:

  • Users would like the front-end design of the Invoices within the claim screen to display the original months claimed and the associated accounting period, alongside the most up-to-date months and associated accounting period as this would enable them to get an 'at-a-glance' view of the months left for an individual invoice if a query was received by DHSC. Instead of having to go into each individual resubmission to find the invoice and their latest value, they could access this screen and get that information easily. ** This design is yet to be implemented. **
  • Users told us confirmed that they would not click the "View entitlement details" or person record link (whichever was included) on the Add invoices to resubmission screen. They would only access this information if they were still required to check the invoice's date of birth to check what age bracket they fall into. However, as resubs, going forward, are split into specific age groups, they no longer have to worry about their being two separate invoices for the same person. This link has now been removed. However, the link is included in migrated mixed age group resubmissions as the requirement to check an invoice's date of birth is relevant.
  • Users are not bothered if the button label for "Partially maintain and withdraw" is shortened to just "Partial" as they understand it's meaning either way. This button and it's position has now been changed so that it sits on the same line as the 'Maintain' and 'Withdraw' buttons.
  • Users struggle with entering the revised accounting period when partially maintaining and withdrawing months. This is a completely new process for them. However, the service has determined that guidance and training will reduce confusion and instill confidence.
  • Similarly to the above, half of users are unsure how to change the period of maintained and withdrawn months within the Partial - Check your answers screen. But when talking it through, and realising that the system calculates these two periods based on the revised accounting period that they input, they would know in future that this is how to change those periods if they didn't look correct. Again, this is another element which can be solved with guidance and training on the new system.
  • Users would like to see the remaining amount of the claim on the Resubmission summary screen as they're unable to access the documents before or during peer review to check the latest claim value on the letter that is sent to the Member State. Being able to see this value would give users greater confidence that the resub they're working on now, logically follows the previous resub. ** This is yet to be implemented. **
  • Users felt confused by the content "Total number of months remaining" on the Claim summary screen as they associate the word "Claimed" with the original value. This has now been changed to "Remaining total number of months" as users felt that the word "remaining" or "current" is clearer that it signifies the toal months left in the claim after a resub has been made.

Any known issues:

  • The "Number of maintained invoices, including those partially maintained" and "Number of withdrawn invoices, including those partially withdrawn" on the Resubmission summary screen does not make sense. You cannot maintain or withdraw the invoice itself, rather it's the months being claimed in their name. This causes confusion when the number of maintained and withdrawn invoices exceeds the total number of contested invoices due to 1 invoice being split across both maintained and withdrawn if they are partially maintained and withdrawn. Therefore, this content has been removed from the resub summary altogether as it provides no values to users.

Version 37 / S1/S072 Average cost UK Claims, Contestations and Resubmissions
(April - Beginning of May 2025)

S1/S072 Average cost claims (MVP Version 1)
- Interaction Designer: Megan Bell In this version you will find the warning screen to prevent Main claims with the same criteria being run twice has been removed, instead this has been entirely blocked using validation and error messaging. You will also find the first and second iteration of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions.

For site flows, wireframes, and general design thinking for S1 Claims (MVP Version 1) visit the Content and Design Miro board (Password 'NHSBSA2025').

What we created:

  • Validation and error messaging which blocks users from running a Main claim with the same criteria twice.
  • The first iteration of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions.
  • The second iteration of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions which includes smaller tweaks to content and design as opposed to huge changes to user flows, navigation, etc.

What we plan to test:

  • Validation and error messaging which blocks users from running a Main claim with the same criteria twice.
  • The first iteration of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions.
  • The second iteration of Average Cost Contestations and Resubmissions with focus on the changes we made as a result of feedback from users during our first round of user research.
Begin these journeys here by searching for an S1/S072 UK claim.

What we found:

  • Users had no issues creating a new resubmission.
  • On the 'Confirmation resubmission created' screen, we originally included links for users to download both the Acknowledgement Letter and Resubmission Summary documents. However, users told us that they are highly unlikely to do anything with these documnets at the point of creating a new resubmission. They also said that the acknowledgement letter would either be sent on RINA (for RINA contestations) or Office Support would send the acknowledgement letter when they receive a postal contestation, therefore, the need for Claims Officers to download the acknowledgement letter from the Ops system is not relevant. This meant that we were able to remove the two links to said documents, and during the second round of usability testing, users told us that the confirmation screen was now clearer and direct, and that they would expect to find the Acknowledgement Letter and Resubmission Summary under the 'Resubmission documents' tab, within an individual resubmission.
  • Users are aware of the various resubmission statuses, though tend to only use the first few (e.g., To action, In progress, To review) to guide them throughout the resubmission process. Users did say, however, that they wouldn't rely solely on the resubmission statuses but would also use the resubmission ID, or the position in which it falls in a table (e.g., the most recently created/worked on resubmission would be at the top of the table). Users also said that if there were several resubmissions within one claim, they may use the filter to view resubmissions under 'To action' to locate the most recently created resubmission(s) to add invoices to.
  • Users do not mind having to manually select invoices to add to a resubmission and even said, they couldn't think of another way to do this at this stage. They also highlighted how useful the check your answers - style screen is in terms of double checking they have selected the correct invoices to be added to the resubmission, with emphasis on the ability to add more invoices or remove any added by mistake. They also like the summary below the page heading which clearly tells them the total amount of months and invoices added to the resubmission. Furthermore, users also pointed out that our decision to split claims up across the different age groups makes selecting invoices easier as they no longer need to worry about one invoice being in a resub more than once - avoiding confusion.
  • Users are generally happy with the information included in the invoices table when adding invoices to a resubmission, highlighting that too much information would clutter the screen and become unhelpful. While they're used to need to check the invoice's personal details to check their date of birth in their person record on MedBen, users stated that because the Ops system will divide claims into age groups, they would no longer feel the need to check an invoices' personal details as they would expect the system to correctly pull the right invoices into the correct claim i.e., using their date of birth to determine which age groups the invoice should fall into when creating a new claim. If users did still feel the need to check the invoices' personal or entitlement details, they would navigate out of the resubmission and go to Search to find the person's record.
  • Users said they're pleased they are able to create numerous resubmissions within one claim so that multiple Claims Officers can work on different contestations in parallel with one another. They pointed out that the system should not be able to pull in invoices already in an in-progress resubmission though as this has the potential to cause conflict in the totals. For example, if one Claim Officer set Jane Doe as 2 months withdrawn but another Claims Officer set her as fully maintained, the system and therefore users would become confused.
  • Users stated their preference for the order in which resubmissions appear on the Claim Resubmissions tab, stating that resubmissions should be ordered in state of completion i.e., the most recently completed should be at the top of the table, with the first complete resubmission at the bottom. The preference stems from users' need to know if there was a previous resubmission and the total number of invoices and months maintained/withdrawn or partially maintained and withdrawn. They said not knowing the most recently completed resubmission could cause issues as it becomes time consuming having to go into each resubmission to work out who actioned what and why.
  • Users were at first, unsure about the 'Withdrawal letter' but after explaining this is only associated with a Credit Note, users understood what the Withdrawal Letter document contains and when it would be available to download from the 'Resubmission documents' tab within the individual resubmission.
  • When investigating individual invoices and deciding whether to Maintain/Withdraw or Partially maintain and withdraw costs from the 'Invoice details', users would still want to view the invoice's person record and entitlement details to double check the information they're viewing is up-to-date and accurate, this is especially important if an entitlement has been cancelled during or after the claim was generated and sent to the Member State. Users said they would go into the Case history - Entitlement history to view the date the entitlement was cancelled to help them work out the timeline of events, and subsequently make a decision. Users also pointed out that they would also want to check a person's notes in instances where information may have been added about their entitlement details such as any updates/changes, as these notes can provide context which helps inform their decisions. Users stated they would prefer a direct link to an invoice's person record as navigating to Search and locating the correct individual for each invoice would be very time consuming and lots of clicks. Some users knew how to use the breadcrumbs to navigate out of the resubmission and into Search to look for the person record but their preference is a direct link to the person record.
  • From our first round of research, we found that the 'Reasons for contestation' weren't quite right, with some not fitting the context of the Average Cost journey. Users told us that sometimes postal reasons can be different to RINA reasons and therefore they will use the 'Other' reason and free-type the reason here if there is no pre-defined reason which matches what the Member State has provided. However, the second round of user research found that while there were a lot of reasons matching the S102 from RINA, users said that they may at some point in the future, need to use any of the reasons so it's good they match RINA.
  • The first iteration of reasons for maintaining/withdrawing and partially maintaining and withdrawing matched the S104 Reply to contestation, however, user research found that users were confused to see some reasons in each of the different journeys. For example, they didn't expect to see 'Contestation accepted in full and IMO concerned is decreased.' within the Maintain journey as this reason means that the UK are accepting the Member State's contestation and fully withdrawing the entire months claimed. However, we knew that each reason would need surfaced and placed into the correct journey but want user and stakeholder feedback in order to do this. In the second round of user research, users noted a marked improvement and as they were going through each reason in the different pathways, found each reason in the correct pathway.
